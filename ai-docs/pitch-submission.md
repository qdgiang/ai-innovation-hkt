**EverMind — organizational memory that runs itself**

EverMind is an ambient institutional-memory and coordination system for volunteer organizations. It listens where the team already talks, turns conversation into an auditable graph of decisions and tasks, and returns that memory as weekly digests, answers-with-receipts, and early warnings — without asking a single volunteer to change how they work.

**The problem.** NPOs run on group chat, and chat forgets. Weekly status is compiled by hand. Blockers surface after they have already cost a week. Decisions get re-explained to every newcomer because the "why" is buried in scrollback. And because volunteers rotate, knowledge leaves with people — vendor contacts, account access, last year's reasoning. The organization does not lack communication; it lacks memory with authority.

**The core idea — three design moves.**

1. Decisions are the write path; tasks are the projection. Every change to work is captured as an append-only decision: who decided, when, the rationale, which tasks it affects, and citations to the exact chat messages it came from. Decisions are never edited — a new one supersedes the old, which stays one click away. The live task board is simply the fold of currently-effective decisions plus assignees' own progress updates. This single choice yields time-travel (open any task → its full decision trail with receipts), safe correction (veto a wrong decision and whatever it displaced is automatically restored), and honest answers ("Why did we switch to LED lanterns?" → the decision, its rationale, the superseded alternative, the source messages).

2. Zero new habits. A bot joins the existing group chats; an AI pass runs over each batch of ~100 messages (configurable), extracting proposed decisions, progress updates, and weak signals. Meetings enter via transcript upload, with speaker names mapped to members. Optional markers (!decision, !blocked, !progress) give power users a deterministic, AI-free fast lane. Approvals happen the way people already talk — replying "ok, decided" or tapping 👍. If EverMind disappears tomorrow, coordination continues exactly as before: additive, never load-bearing.

3. Authority is the safety model. The org chart — coordinator, team leads, members — is data the write path enforces. A member's message can only ever create a proposal; it becomes effective when sufficient rank approves; nobody can supersede a decision made above their rank; assignees update their own progress freely (the one frictionless lane). This is also the AI-safety mechanism: the LLM can only propose, never decide. Low-confidence extractions wait for one-tap human confirmation even from authorized speakers. Precision comes from workflow, not just prompting.

**What the organization gets.** A per-team Monday digest computed from records — every line cited, the lead's own weekly wrap quoted alongside. Cited Q&A that is supersession-aware and knows "no class this Sunday" is a windowed exception, not a schedule change. A blocker radar that groups external waits by counterparty ("three teams are waiting on the same vendor"), tracks cross-team and cross-project dependencies, and flags stale and idle work — deterministic SQL, pushed daily, escalating to the person who can actually act. And rotation solved in both directions: newcomers get an onboarding brief the moment they join; a departure triggers a sweep of every task, approval, and external relationship that person holds.

**Under the hood.** A platform-generic chat contract (send, reply, react, edit, media, membership) behind thin adapters. Threshold-triggered extraction windows with reply-target hydration (a terse "ok, decided" reply resolves against a weeks-old proposal) and a persistent signal ledger, so a blocker mentioned in passing three times across separate weeks is caught even though no single mention would justify a record. The world model matches NPO reality: dated campaigns with close-out and retrospectives, ongoing programs with no false deadlines, one chat group per project, matrix team membership, and first-class external parties.

**Why the demo can be trusted.** Every record carries citations pinned to message revisions — receipts, not paraphrase. The bot maintains or withdraws everything it announces: retractions thread to the original post, extraction backlogs are disclosed, a withdrawn "you're unblocked" is followed by its inverse. Every AI feature has a deterministic sibling. The design was hardened through 43 adversarial end-to-end scenarios (a 59-gap register — every gap fixed or explicitly accepted with rationale), and ships with a hand-verified synthetic corpus whose answer key doubles as the extraction-quality gate.

**Built to be handed off.** One container plus one managed database (~$5/month), an OpenAI-compatible LLM behind a config line, redeploy-button operations for a non-technical team. The tool is disposable; the memory is not.
